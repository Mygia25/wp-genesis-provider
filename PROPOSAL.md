## 1. BRAND IDENTITY PROPOSAL

### 1.1 Color Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| Primary | Genesis Teal | `#00D1A0` | CTAs, accents, highlights, icon fills |
| Primary Dark | Deep Forest | `#004D40` | Section backgrounds, dark overlays, footer |
| Accent | Soft Teal | `#B2F0E3` | Hover states, badges, light highlights |
| Background | Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | Page background, card bases |
| Surface | Warm Gray | `#F7F9F8` | Section alternates, input fields |
| Text Primary | Near Black | `#1C1C1C` | Headlines, body copy |
| Text Secondary | Slate Gray | `#4A5568` | Subtext, descriptions, captions |
| Dividers | Light Border | `#E2E8F0` | Card borders, horizontal rules |
| Success | Affirm Green | `#38A169` | Confirmations, availability indicators |
| Alert | Warm Amber | `#F6AD55` | Callout boxes, important notices |

**Color Strategy:** The teal-on-white palette communicates cleanliness and care — it reads as medical-adjacent but warmer and more human than clinical blue. Deep Forest anchors sections and creates strong contrast without feeling cold. The palette is accessible at WCAG AA+ on all combinations.

### 1.2 Typography

**Heading Font:** `Nunito` (Google Fonts)
- Weights used: 700 (H1/H2), 600 (H3/H4), 800 (hero emphasis)
- Why: Rounded letterforms feel approachable and friendly without losing professionalism. Common in healthcare and community-service brands.
- Import: `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Nunito:wght@400;600;700;800&display=swap`

**Body Font:** `Inter` (Google Fonts)
- Weights used: 400 (body), 500 (labels/nav), 600 (buttons)
- Why: Best-in-class screen legibility. Neutral, modern, and universally trusted. Pairs with Nunito's warmth to balance personality with readability.
- Import: `https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@400;500;600&display=swap`

**Type Scale (Desktop):**

### 1.3 Logo Concept Direction

**Concept Name:** "The Genesis Mark"

**Concept Description:**
A wordmark-plus-icon combination that works at all sizes — from favicon to billboard.

**Primary Mark:**
- Icon: A stylized open hand cradling a small leaf or seedling, rendered in `#00D1A0`. The hand represents care, human connection, and the personal assistant nature of the service. The leaf signals growth, life, and healing.
- Wordmark: "Genesis" in Nunito 700, `#1C1C1C` — full weight, confident. "Provider Services" in Inter 500, `#4A5568` — smaller, stacked beneath.
- Optional: A thin teal horizontal line connects the icon to the wordmark for horizontal lockups.

**Icon Variations:**
- Full horizontal lockup (primary): icon left, text right
- Stacked lockup (square formats): icon above, text below
- Icon only (favicon, app icon, social avatar)
- Reversed (white on dark teal for footer)

**Logo Don'ts:**
- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not use icon without wordmark at sizes below 120px wide
- Do not place on busy photo backgrounds without a white background box
- Minimum size: 80px wide (horizontal lockup)

**Deliverable Format:** SVG (master), PNG (2x transparent), PNG (white reversed), Favicon ICO

### 1.4 Visual Style

**Photography Direction:**
- Real people, not perfect. Warm-toned lifestyle photography showing caregivers and their recipients in genuine moments — not staged stock photos.
- Scenes: Home settings (living rooms, kitchens, gardens), never clinical spaces unless showing a care moment.
- Diversity: Black, Hispanic, and white subjects of varying ages. Caregivers shown as warm professionals — dressed neatly but not in scrubs unless contextually appropriate.
- Lighting: Soft, natural window light. Golden hour tones. No flat or harsh overhead.
- Composition: Medium and close shots. Eye contact with camera or meaningful interaction. Authentic expressions.

**AI Image Style Notes (Midjourney/Flux prompt style):**
"Candid lifestyle photography, natural window lighting, warm tones, photorealistic, shallow depth of field, 35mm lens, editorial quality, NOT stock photo, NOT perfect, authentic emotion"

**Icon Style:**
- Line icons, 2px stroke, rounded caps — Phosphor Icons or Heroicons outline set
- Color: Teal `#00D1A0` on white backgrounds; white on dark backgrounds
- Size: 40x40px in cards, 24x24px in lists
- Do NOT mix styles — all icons must be from the same family

**Illustration / Decoration:**
- Minimal. Occasional soft teal blob shapes or organic curves as section dividers
- No clipart, no cartoon illustrations, no gradients that feel "app-ish"
- White space is intentional and generous

## 3. DESIGN DIRECTION

### 3.1 Three Mood/Style Options

---

**Option A — "Warm Modern"**

**Aesthetic:** Clean white backgrounds with generous padding. Soft teal accents. Large, expressive typography. Organic shapes as decorative elements (gentle blob dividers between sections). Photos are full-width with subtle overlays. Icons are outline-style in teal. Sections alternate between white and warm gray (`#F7F9F8`).

**Feel:** Approachable, human, trustworthy. Looks modern but not cold. Feels like a brand that knows its audience.

**Best for:** Home page hero, services cards, testimonials.

**Comparable brands (aesthetic only):** Calm app, Nuvei Health, Kindred at Home.

---

**Option B — "Bold & Confident"**

**Aesthetic:** Deep Forest (`#004D40`) section backgrounds with white and teal text. Bold typography — larger, louder headlines. Strong contrast. Full-bleed photo sections with heavy teal overlay. Geometric graphic elements (thin lines, bold borders). Icons are filled, not outline.

**Feel:** Authoritative, established, serious. Signals professionalism and strength.

**Best for:** About Us hero, Why Choose Us, credential sections.

**Comparable brands (aesthetic only):** Kindred Healthcare, Accolade, larger health systems.

---

**Option C — "Minimal Light"**

**Aesthetic:** Almost entirely white. Maximum white space. Very fine typography. Teal used only as a single accent (buttons, one headline word, underlines). No background colors. Cards float with subtle box shadows. Photography is small and editorial.

**Feel:** Premium, boutique, modern. Feels more like a design agency than a care provider — which could stand out in this market.

**Best for:** If client wants to signal high-end positioning.

**Risk:** May feel too cold for an audience that needs warmth and accessibility.

---

### 3.2 RECOMMENDED OPTION

**Recommendation: Option A — "Warm Modern"** with select elements from Option B.

**Justification:**
The target audience — elderly and disabled individuals and their families — prioritizes trust, warmth, and legibility over aesthetic trend. Option A delivers a clean, modern look that will outcompete the dated 2017-era competitor sites, while maintaining the emotional warmth that Charlene's brand promise demands. Borrowing Option B's deep teal for footer and one or two section backgrounds adds authority without sacrificing approachability. The result is a site that looks like it belongs in 2026, while still feeling human.
